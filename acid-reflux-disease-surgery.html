Acid Reflux Disease Surgery

Gastroesophageal reflux disease, also known as GERD, or the less-severe acid reflux disease cause people around the world pain through heartburn, regurgitation and indigestion. Mild cases of heartburn or acid reflux disease can be treated with over-the-counter or prescription medication, but severe cases require surgery.

The best acid reflux surgery is laparoscopic nissen fundoplication, a proven and effective procedure to stop acid from flowing up the esophagus. If you're struggling with acid reflux disease or GERD, please call (470) 419-4380 or contact us online.

What is Acid Reflux Disease?

Acid reflux disease and GERD are chronic digestive diseases. These disorders afflict the stomach and esophagus; they are caused by stomach acid flowing back up through the esophagus. Some of the common symptoms of GERD include:

  • Vomiting
  • Regurgitation
  • Difficult swallowing
  • Sore throat
  • Nausea
  • Chest Pain
  • Coughing

GERD itself is caused by any number of factors, including some of them hereditary in nature. Other causes of acid reflux disease include:

  • Obesity
  • Diabetes
  • Asthma
  • Dry mouth
  • Cigarette smoking
  • Pregnancy
  • Hernias

With most cases of GERD and acid reflux disease, it is recommended to seek treatment. For severe cases, there is no better acid reflux disease treatment than laparoscopic nissen fundoplication

What is Nissen Fundoplication Surgery?

Laparoscopic nissen fundoplication, sometimes shortened to “Lap Nissen” and named after the doctor that created it, is the premier surgery for acid reflux disease and GERD. As the name implies, this surgery is performed laparoscopically, meaning it is minimally invasive and performed through only a small incision.

During the surgery itself, the upper curve of the stomach (also known as the fundus) is wrapped around the esophagus and attached with a strong mesh. By doing this, acid and bile can no longer flow back up through the esophagus.

The surgery also promotes the healing of the esophagus, which is regularly damaged by GERD and acid reflux disease. The lap nissen surgery has proven to be effective, with most who receive it seeing a noticeable decrease or complete elimination of GERD symptoms.

Acid Reflux Disease Surgery Recovery

Because nissen fundoplication is typically formed laparoscopically, the recovery time is short. In most instances, the actual recovery time is mere days and most normal activities can be resumed a short time after that. During the healing process, a special lap nissen diet may be recommended to help ease esophagus pain and promote healthy recovery.
